Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, including business contact information processed in the course of providing services.
Processing: Any operation performed on personal data, including collection, storage, use, transmission, and deletion.
Controller: The client who determines the purposes and means of processing personal data.
Processor: AutoUp, which processes personal data on behalf of the Controller.
Subprocessor: Any third party engaged by AutoUp to assist in the processing of personal data.
2. Scope and Purpose
AutoUp processes personal data solely for the purpose of providing outbound email campaign services as described in the service agreement. The categories of data processed include: business contact information (names, email addresses, job titles, company details), campaign engagement data (opens, clicks, replies), and any additional data fields provided by the Controller for campaign personalization.
3. Processor Obligations

4. Subprocessors
AutoUp uses subprocessors to assist in providing services, including email sending infrastructure providers, data enrichment services, and analytics platforms. AutoUp maintains a current list of subprocessors, available upon request. AutoUp will notify the Controller of any intended changes to subprocessors, providing the Controller an opportunity to object. 5. International Transfers

6. Data Breach Notification
In the event of a personal data breach, AutoUp will notify the Controller without undue delay and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach. The notification will include: a description of the nature of the breach, including categories and approximate number of data subjects affected; the name and contact details of the point of contact for further information; a description of the likely consequences of the breach; and a description of measures taken or proposed to address the breach.
7. Data Subject Requests

8. Audit Rights
The Controller may audit AutoUp's compliance with this DPA upon reasonable notice (no less than 30 days). Audits shall be conducted during business hours, no more than once per year, and at the Controller's expense. AutoUp may satisfy audit requests by providing relevant certifications, audit reports, or other documentation.
9. Duration and Termination
This DPA remains in effect for the duration of the service agreement between AutoUp and the Controller. Upon termination, AutoUp will delete all personal data processed on behalf of the Controller within 90 days, unless retention is required by law or the Controller requests data return in a standard format.
10. Liability
Each party's liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations of liability set forth in the Terms of Use and the applicable service agreement.
